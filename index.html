About the Conference

Where computing, communication & intelligent sensing meet in the Himalayas

2026 IEEE NEPCON is the international signature conference of the IEEE Nepal Section, gathering researchers, academicians, industry professionals, and practitioners to present and discuss the latest advancements, innovations, and challenges in emerging computing and engineering domains — from computing systems and communication technologies to intelligent sensing and digital solutions. The program spans keynote addresses, peer-reviewed technical sessions, and academia–industry collaboration.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

How do I submit a paper?

All papers are submitted electronically through the ConfConnects portal using the official IEEE conference paper template (2-column). The submission deadline is 15 August 2026 — see the Call for Papers.

Will accepted papers be published in IEEE Xplore?

Accepted, registered, and presented papers are intended for submission to IEEE Xplore, subject to the publisher's approval and IEEE quality review.

Can I present online (virtually)?

Yes, where permitted. Please note that online presenters are not eligible for the conference kit or other on-site physical materials. See Registration.

Are registration fees refundable?

No. All registration fees are non-refundable and non-transferable once paid. Please review the Refund Policy before registering.

How many registrations are required per paper?

At least one author of each accepted paper must complete a full author registration by 20 October 2026 for the paper to be presented and included in the proceedings.

Do I need a visa to attend?

Many nationalities can obtain a visa on arrival at Tribhuvan International Airport (KTM); please verify your country's requirements in advance. Contact the organizing committee for an official invitation letter — see Venue.
